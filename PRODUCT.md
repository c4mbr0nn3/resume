# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Hiring managers, engineering leaders, and technical recruiters** evaluating Francesco Zorzi as a candidate. They visit the site to quickly assess experience, skills, and professional fit — typically in the context of reviewing applicants, preparing for an interview, or comparing candidates side-by-side. The secondary audience is peers and collaborators who want a concise, well-presented professional summary.

Users arrive with limited time and a scanning mindset. They need to find key signals (roles, technologies, tenure, impact) fast, without friction. The job to be done: *"Can I tell within 30 seconds whether this person is worth a deeper look?"*

## Product Purpose

A personal resume site that presents Francesco Zorzi's professional profile in a clear, trustworthy, and memorable way. It exists to make a strong first impression — conveying competence and taste through both *what* is presented and *how* it is presented. Success means the visitor leaves with a clear, positive sense of the person behind the resume.

## Brand Personality

**Precise · Warm · Confident**

The tone is direct but not cold, refined but not precious. Professional the way a thoughtful colleague who respects your time — not the way a corporation presents itself. The design communicates: *I care about craft, I know what matters, and I won't waste your attention.*

Voice: declarative, confident, understated. Avoids hype, jargon, and self-congratulation.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — Overused Tailwind/Astro starter look, identical card grids, hero-metric template.
- **Over-designed agency** — Loud gradients, heavy animations, excessive flair, parallax-for-the-sake-of-it.
- **Corporate LinkedIn clone** — Boring, sterile, default professional. Navy/gold finance templates.

The site should feel like a well-made printed CV that happens to be on screen — not like a web app.

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration.** Every element earns its place. No gratuitous embellishment, side-stripe borders, or decorative flourishes. Restraint is the signal of quality.

2. **Warm professionalism.** Approachable, not sterile. The indigo palette leans warm; the serif type brings human texture. Avoids cold corporate blues and grays.

3. **Typography-forward identity.** Spectral (serif) carries the personality — it's the hero. Work Sans (sans) provides structure. Let type contrast and scale do the layout work. Hierarchy through weight and scale, not boxes.

4. **Confidence through restraint.** The site doesn't need to shout. No big numbers, no stats, no hero metrics. A tight composition with generous white space communicates more than any embellishment.

5. **Content-first, always.** The resume data drives the layout. Print-like information hierarchy: name → role → contact → summary → experience → skills. Each section exists to answer the next question a recruiter has.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

General best practices: keyboard-navigable, sufficient color contrast (meeting WCAG AA), readable font sizes, reduced-motion-friendly. No specific additional requirements identified.
